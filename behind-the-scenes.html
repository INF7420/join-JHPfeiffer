My Story

There was once a young mom who lived in a town that she did not grow up in or work in. She only lived there for a short time when she made the decision to become a stay at home mother. This mom became very sad and lonely and felt trapped at home with a colicy infant. She missed her co-workers and her professional life, so she started to look for opportunities for herself and her child. This turn out to be a very difficult task but she finally located a dated news article about a local mothers group. It took her several months to build up her confidence to attend a meeting, she even did a couple drive-bys. Finally, the day came when she made herself walk through the door and attend her first meeting. It was on this day her whole life changed! Suddenly, she was surrounded by other parents who knew about playgroups, story times, and kid events. This mom met other mothers who became her friends, she joined the parent group, and she started going to other events in her area. This young mother started to feel happy, she did not feel trapped at home anymore, and she started to enjoy motherhood.

This young mom knew that other parents were feeling like she had and wanted to reach out to them. She decided that she needed to create a website that informed parents in the Great Lakes Bay Region of the wealth of resources available to them. Six years later this mom continues to reach out to other parents with the hope that she can help them find the resources they need in those lonely early years.

Timeline

  • 2012

    3mommies.com launched by three stay-at-home mothers with the mission to provide information to families in the Great Lakes Bay Region.

    Greatlakesbaymoms.com launched by a local business and mothers with the mission to provide information to families in the Great Lakes Bay Region.

    3mommies.com and Greatlakesbaymoms.com collaborated resources and created a partnership that became Great Lakes Bay Moms, LLC.

    The website grew to include a Kids Calendar, Blog Post, and Directories. Social media pages on Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram were open to drive users to the website.

    Group members were interviewed by MLive and made monthly appearances on WNEM’s morning show.

    The first Annual Kids Block Party hosted by GLBM occurred in Bay City. It featured activities, crafts, performances, and businesses. It was sponsored by local businesses including the Catholic Federal Credit Union, Covenant HealthCare, Dow Bay Area Family Y, McLaren Hospital, car dealership and Walmart.

  • 2013

    The Second Annual Kids Block Party hosted by GLBM occurred in Bay City.

    Collaborations were formed with the local Great Start groups and businesses.

    Group members attended local kids events to promote the business.

    Bloggers were paid for their contributions to the website.

  • 2014

    The Third Annual Kids Block Party hosted by GLBM occurred in Bay City.

  • 2015

    Great Lakes Bay Moms, LLC’s three controlling partners dispersed and the business was dissolved. The website’s ownership was given to one partner.

  • 2019

    Great Lakes Bay Parents, LLC launched.
